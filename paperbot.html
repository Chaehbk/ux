Chaehyun Baek

PaperBot

Summary

PaperBot is an edutainment paper toy that can be assembled without a scissors, glue or chemical. Kids can create their own animal-shaped toys and add up various physical movements using the electronic module kits. PaperBot was designed for early science education to help children to understand how electronic devices work and let them make their own easy and safely.

Role

  • Designed three types of PaperBots and paper assebly kits - Bear, Crocodile, Parrot
  • Developed scale mockups and interactive prototypes
  • Worked cross-functionally with design, engineering, and project management
  • Launched workshop and demonstrated PaperBots at Maker Faire in Seoul, 2015

Details

PaperBot provides a set of cardboard and electronic block modules including a power and various types of input/output modules.

First, the kid need to create a short story using an "if" conditional statement for the PaperBot. Then, choose the electronic modules following their scenario.

"If I touch my crocodile's head, he will growl and bite my hand"
